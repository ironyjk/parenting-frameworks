# Project-Based Learning (PBL) — Theoretical Foundation

## Overview

Project-Based Learning (PBL) is a teaching method in which students learn by actively engaging in real-world and personally meaningful projects. Unlike projects assigned after teaching (dessert projects), PBL IS the teaching — the project drives the learning.

---

## 7 Essential Design Elements (Gold Standard PBL)

PBLWorks (formerly Buck Institute for Education) defines these. ALL seven must be present:

### 1. Challenging Problem or Question
The **Driving Question** is the engine. Must be open-ended, engaging, aligned, understandable.

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Philosophical | "Should we...?" | "Should our city ban single-use plastics?" |
| Product/Design | "How can we design...?" | "How can we design a water filter from local materials?" |
| Role-based | "How would a ___ solve...?" | "How would an urban planner redesign school traffic?" |
| Problem-based | "How can we solve...?" | "How can we reduce cafeteria food waste by 50%?" |

### 2. Sustained Inquiry
Multi-week investigation. Students generate sub-questions. Research is purposeful and iterative.

### 3. Authenticity
Levels: simulated scenario -> real tools -> real audience -> real impact.

### 4. Student Voice and Choice
Spectrum from teacher-defined options to student co-design.

### 5. Reflection
Ongoing, structured, metacognitive. Not just at the end.

### 6. Critique and Revision
Gallery walk, tuning protocol, Austin's Butterfly, rubric-based peer review.
**Rules:** Be kind, be specific, be helpful.

### 7. Public Product
Shared beyond the classroom: presentations, websites, prototypes, exhibitions.

---

## PBL vs. "Doing Projects"

| "Doing Projects" | PBL |
|---|---|
| Assigned AFTER teaching | IS the vehicle for teaching |
| Teacher-directed | Student-driven with scaffolding |
| Decorative product | Authentic product addressing real need |
| 2-3 days | 2-6 weeks (sustained) |
| Content could be learned without it | Content learned THROUGH the project |

---

## Scaffolding

### Before
- Entry event (provocative launch)
- Know/Need to Know list
- Driving question unpacking
- Group norms and contracts

### During
- Mini-lessons (just-in-time)
- Workshops (longer skill-building)
- Check-ins and benchmarks
- Structured critique protocols

### After
- Public presentation
- Individual reflection
- Team debrief
- Teacher reflection

---

## Teacher's Role

1. **Designer** — project framework, driving question, scaffolding plan
2. **Facilitator** — manage inquiry, ask probing questions
3. **Coach** — targeted feedback, model expert thinking
4. **Curator** — connect to resources, experts, authentic audiences
5. **Assessor** — formative assessment, actionable feedback

---

## Assessment

### Individual Accountability
Reflection journals, individual quizzes, peer evaluation of contribution, individual components.

### Rubric Design
**Process:** Research quality, collaboration, iteration, time management.
**Product:** Content accuracy, reasoning, creativity, communication, audience impact.

---

## Driving Question Design

1. Start with learning goals
2. Identify real-world context
3. Find the tension (problem, debate, challenge)
4. Frame as a question
5. Test: Not Googleable? Leads to goals? Interesting? Yields meaningful product?
6. Iterate with colleagues and students
