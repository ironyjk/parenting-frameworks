# Project-Based Learning (PBL) Skill

> "Tell me and I forget. Teach me and I remember. Involve me and I learn." — attributed to Benjamin Franklin

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## Execution Protocol

### 1. Design the Driving Question
Open-ended, engaging, aligned to learning goals, not Googleable.

### 2. Plan the Project (7 Essential Elements Checklist)
- [ ] Challenging Problem or Question
- [ ] Sustained Inquiry (multi-week)
- [ ] Authenticity (real-world context/impact)
- [ ] Student Voice and Choice
- [ ] Reflection (ongoing, structured)
- [ ] Critique and Revision (peer feedback cycles)
- [ ] Public Product (audience beyond classroom)

### 3. Scaffold the Process

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|-----------|
| **Launch** | Entry event, Know/Need-to-Know list, group norms |
| **Build** | Mini-lessons (just-in-time), workshops, research, prototyping |
| **Check** | Benchmarks, formative checkpoints, peer critique sessions |
| **Refine** | Revision based on feedback, rehearsal/dry run |
| **Present** | Public presentation to authentic audience |
| **Reflect** | Individual reflection, team debrief |

### 4. Formative Checkpoints

| Week | Checkpoint | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Know/Need-to-Know + sub-questions | Understand the driving question |
| 1-2 | Research plan + source evaluation | Quality research happening |
| 2-3 | Rough draft / Prototype v1 | Catch misconceptions early |
| 3-4 | Peer critique session | External feedback |
| 4-5 | Rehearsal / dry run | Refine before final |
| 5-6 | Final presentation + reflection | Summative |

### 5. Assess Both Process and Product

**Process:** Research quality, collaboration, iteration, time management
**Product:** Content accuracy, reasoning, creativity, communication, impact

Ensure individual accountability: journals, quizzes, peer evaluation, individual components.

---

## Anti-patterns

1. **Dessert projects** — assigning after teaching, not as the vehicle
2. **No driving question** — projects without a central question become activity lists
3. **Teacher-directed "projects"** — step-by-step removes inquiry and voice
4. **No scaffolding** — expecting self-direction without support
5. **All group, no individual** — no accountability mechanisms
6. **Skipping critique** — going straight to final without feedback
7. **Low authenticity** — products only the teacher sees
8. **Content-free** — fun without substantive learning
9. **One-shot assessment** — only grading the final product
10. **Ignoring reflection** — moving on without processing
