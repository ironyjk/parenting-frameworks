# Positive Discipline Skill

> "Where did we ever get the crazy idea that in order to make children do better, first we have to make them feel worse?" — Jane Nelsen

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

## Execution Protocol

### 1. Identify the Mistaken Goal
Check YOUR emotional reaction to find the child's coded message:
- **Annoyed** -> Undue Attention -> "Notice me. Involve me."
- **Angry/challenged** -> Misguided Power -> "Let me help. Give me choices."
- **Hurt** -> Revenge -> "I'm hurting. Validate my feelings."
- **Hopeless** -> Assumed Inadequacy -> "Don't give up on me."

### 2. Curiosity Questions (instead of lectures)

```
Parent: "What happened?"
Child:  "She took my toy."
Parent: "How did that make you feel?"
Child:  "Mad."
Parent: "What did you do when you felt mad?"
Child:  "I hit her."
Parent: "How did that work out for you?"
Child:  "Not good."
Parent: "What could you try next time instead?"
```

**Rules:** Ask only when calm. Genuine curiosity, not sarcasm. Wait for the child to think.

### 3. Logical Consequences (4 R's: Related, Respectful, Reasonable, Revealed)

```
Parent: "We agreed bikes go in the garage. Yours was in the driveway. 
         What was our agreement?"
Child:  "Put it in the garage."
Parent: "And what happens when it's left out?"
Child:  "I don't ride it the next day."
Parent: "Right. You can ride again day after tomorrow. I trust you'll 
         remember next time."
```

### 4. Family Meetings (weekly, 15-30 min)

1. **Compliments/Appreciations** — each person appreciates someone
2. **Review previous solutions** — did they work?
3. **Agenda items** — anyone can add problems during the week
4. **Problem-solving** — brainstorm -> choose -> try for one week
5. **Plan something fun**

### 5. Positive Time-Out (Cool-Down Space)

NOT a punishment. A self-regulation space the child helps design.

```
Parent: "Sometimes when we're upset, it helps to have a calm-down spot.
         Want to make one together? What would help you feel better 
         there? Stuffed animals? Books? Drawing supplies?"
```

The child goes by choice, not by command.

## Anti-patterns

1. **Kindness without firmness** — becomes permissive
2. **Firmness without kindness** — becomes authoritarian
3. **Curiosity questions when flooded** — wait until both are calm
4. **Consequences that are punishment in disguise** — check the 4 R's
5. **Lecturing during encouragement** — "You did great, BUT..."
6. **Skipping mistaken goal analysis** — treating all misbehavior the same
7. **Expecting perfection** — mistakes are learning opportunities
8. **Family meetings as tribunals** — must be safe, not guilt sessions
