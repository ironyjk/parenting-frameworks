# Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Skill

> "Disability is not in the person; it is in the interaction between the person and the environment." — CAST

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## UDL Implementation Checklist

When designing a lesson or unit, check:

**Engagement (WHY):**
- [ ] Choice in how students engage (topic, tools, grouping, pace)
- [ ] Content connected to students' lives and interests
- [ ] Psychologically safe learning environment
- [ ] Goals clear and visible
- [ ] Feedback growth-oriented and specific

**Representation (WHAT):**
- [ ] Content in more than one format (text + audio + visual)
- [ ] Key vocabulary and symbols pre-taught or supported
- [ ] Background knowledge activated
- [ ] Patterns and big ideas highlighted

**Action and Expression (HOW):**
- [ ] Students can demonstrate learning in more than one way
- [ ] Tools and technology accessible
- [ ] Scaffolds available (and removable)
- [ ] Support for planning and self-monitoring

---

## Practical Strategies by Principle

### Engagement
- Choice boards with 4 ways to explore the same concept
- Let students select which problems to solve
- Begin with a real-world problem students care about
- Flexible seating and workspace options
- Post learning goals visibly
- Tiered tasks at different difficulty levels
- Give feedback: what's working AND what to try next
- Teach self-monitoring checklists

### Representation
- Provide content as text + audio + video + diagram
- Use captions on all videos
- Create visual word walls with images
- Provide graphic organizers showing concept relationships
- Use color-coding to highlight key concepts
- Provide worked examples before independent practice

### Action and Expression
- Allow response via typing, speaking, drawing, or pointing
- Choice of product: essay OR presentation OR podcast OR model
- Provide templates and sentence starters
- Co-create project timelines with students
- Use rubrics shared BEFORE the task

## Anti-patterns

1. **Designing for the average** — there is no average learner
2. **Retrofitting only** — adding accommodations after instead of designing flexibly
3. **Options = lower standards** — multiple PATHS, same high standards
4. **UDL as a checklist** — it's a mindset, not compliance
5. **Technology = UDL** — low-tech options are equally valid
6. **One accommodation fits all** — different learners need different options
7. **Removing all challenge** — support, not an easy path; preserve productive struggle
8. **Ignoring student input** — students know best what helps them learn
9. **UDL only for "special needs"** — benefits ALL learners
10. **Perfection paralysis** — start with one principle and iterate
