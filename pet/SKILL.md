# Parent Effectiveness Training (P.E.T.) Skill

> "The language of unacceptance drives people away. The language of acceptance draws them closer." — Thomas Gordon

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

## Problem Ownership Decision Tree

Ask: **Whose needs are not being met?**

1. **Child's needs unmet** (upset, crying, frustrated) -> **Active Listening**
2. **Parent's needs unmet** (child's behavior tangibly affects parent) -> **I-Message**
3. **Both have unmet needs** (conflict) -> **No-Lose Method**
4. **No one is bothered** -> Enjoy the relationship

**Critical mistake:** Treating the child's problem as your problem.

---

## Tool 1: Active Listening (Child Owns the Problem)

Active Listening IS: reflecting back feelings and meaning so they feel deeply understood.
Active Listening is NOT: advising, reassuring, questioning, lecturing, or distracting.

### Technique
1. **Attend** — Full attention, put everything down
2. **Listen for feelings** — What emotion is underneath?
3. **Reflect** — Feed back feeling + content in YOUR words (not parroting)
4. **Stay with them** — Do not shift to problem-solving until ready

### Example: Child (age 8) comes home upset
```
Child:  "I hate school! I'm never going back!"
Parent: "Sounds like something really bad happened today."
Child:  "Tyler said I'm stupid in front of everyone."
Parent: "That must have been really embarrassing — being called that 
         in front of your classmates."
Child:  "Yeah, and nobody said anything. They all just laughed."
Parent: "So you felt hurt not just by Tyler, but because no one stood 
         up for you."
Child:  [quiet] "Maybe I'll sit with someone else at lunch tomorrow."
```

The parent never advised or fixed. The child processed the emotion and arrived at their own solution.

## Tool 2: I-Messages (Parent Owns the Problem)

### Three-Part Formula
1. **Behavior** (non-blameful): "When [specific observable behavior]..."
2. **Feeling** (genuine emotion): "I feel [emotion]..."
3. **Effect** (tangible impact): "because [specific effect on my life]..."

| You-Message (blaming) | I-Message (owning) |
|---|---|
| "You're so inconsiderate!" | "When music is that loud, I can't concentrate, and I get frustrated." |
| "You never clean up!" | "When I see toys on the stairs, I worry someone will trip." |

### The Shift — I-Message to Active Listening
```
Parent: "When backpacks are dropped in the doorway, I trip over them. 
         It's frustrating."
Child:  "I'm tired when I come home! I have too much homework!"
Parent: (SHIFT) "Sounds like you're exhausted after school."
Child:  "Yes!"
Parent: "You need a break the minute you walk in. I get that. AND I 
         need the doorway clear. Can we figure out a spot that works?"
Child:  "What about the bench by the door?"
Parent: "Perfect."
```

## Tool 3: The No-Lose Method (Method III)

### Six Steps
1. **Define the problem in terms of needs** — "I need..." not "I want you to..."
2. **Generate possible solutions** — Brainstorm without evaluating
3. **Evaluate solutions** — Cross out any either party cannot accept
4. **Choose the best solution** — Consensus, not compromise
5. **Implement** — Who does what, by when?
6. **Check back** — Revisit in a set time

### Example: Bedtime conflict (age 7)
```
Parent: "I need you to get enough sleep. But I can tell bedtime feels 
         too early. What do you need?"
Child:  "I never get to finish my show! And I want to read in bed."
Parent: "So you need time to finish and reading time. Let's brainstorm."
Child:  "Stay up until 10!"
Parent: [writes it down] "Okay, what else?"
Child:  "What if I start getting ready earlier so I have reading time?"
Parent: "Good one. Start at 7:30, reading 8:00-8:30, lights out 8:30?"
Child:  "8:30 is okay if I get the full reading time."
Parent: "Deal. Let's try it for a week. If it's not working, we 
         brainstorm again."
```

## Anti-patterns

1. **Using Active Listening when YOU own the problem** — you'll boil with resentment
2. **Sending I-Messages for values differences** — preferences are not tangible effects
3. **Disguised You-Messages** — "I feel that you're irresponsible" contains a judgment
4. **Method III with a predetermined outcome** — that's Method I with extra steps
5. **Active Listening as interrogation** — reflecting should feel warm, not clinical
6. **Skipping Step 6 (check back)** — solutions decay without follow-up
7. **Mixing Methods** — starting Method III then overriding with Method I
8. **P.E.T. language without P.E.T. attitude** — tools without respect are manipulation
