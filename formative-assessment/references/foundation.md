# Formative Assessment — Theoretical Foundation

## Overview

Formative assessment is the process of gathering evidence about student learning DURING instruction to adjust teaching and improve outcomes. Research by Black and Wiliam (1998) showed that effective formative assessment produces learning gains equivalent to moving an "average" student into the top 35% — one of the largest effect sizes in educational research.

---

## Five Key Strategies (Wiliam, 2011)

|  | Where the learner is going | Where the learner is right now | How to get there |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Teacher** | 1. Clarify learning intentions and success criteria | 2. Engineer tasks that elicit evidence | 3. Provide feedback that moves forward |
| **Peer** | Understand intentions and criteria | 4. Activate students as resources for one another | |
| **Learner** | Understand intentions and criteria | | 5. Activate students as owners of their own learning |

---

## Strategy 1: Clarify Learning Intentions and Success Criteria

- Activity-focused (AVOID): "Read Chapter 5 and answer questions"
- Learning-focused (USE): "Learn how photosynthesis converts sunlight to energy"
- Success criteria: "I can explain the three stages and describe where each occurs"
- Shared BEFORE the lesson; co-constructed when appropriate

## Strategy 2: Engineer Tasks That Elicit Evidence

**Exit Tickets:** 2-3 minute written response targeting the day's learning. Teacher sorts: got it / partially / not yet.

**Hinge Questions:** Carefully designed MC where every wrong answer reveals a specific misconception.

**Think-Pair-Share:** Think (30-60 sec silence), Pair (1-2 min), Share (selected pairs).

**Mini Whiteboards:** ALL students hold up answers simultaneously. No hiding.

**Traffic Lights:** Green/Yellow/Red self-assessment of understanding.

**Random Selection:** Popsicle sticks or random name generator. Every student must think. If "I don't know," scaffold and return.

## Strategy 3: Feedback That Moves Forward

**Effective feedback is:** Specific, actionable, timely, task-focused (not person), forward-looking.

**Does NOT work:** Grades alone, grades + comments (students ignore comments), vague praise, too much feedback.

**Comment-Only Marking:** Provide only written comments. Students read and respond. Grade comes later.

**Structures:** Stars and Stairs, Two Stars and a Wish, Medal and Mission, Glow and Grow.

## Strategy 4: Students as Resources for One Another

**Two Stars and a Wish (Peer):** Partner identifies two strengths and one suggestion, referencing criteria.

**Gallery Walk:** Post work, peers leave specific feedback on sticky notes.

**C3B4ME:** Consult three classmates before asking the teacher.

## Strategy 5: Students as Owners of Their Own Learning

**Self-Assessment Against Criteria:** Same rubric teacher uses. Compare, discuss discrepancies.

**Learning Journals:** "What did I learn? What am I confused about? What will I do about it?"

**Goal Setting:** Specific, measurable learning goals tracked weekly.

---

## The Formative Assessment Cycle

```
1. CLARIFY (learning intention + success criteria)
    -> 2. ELICIT (evidence of understanding)
    -> 3. INTERPRET (what do they understand/misunderstand?)
    -> 4. ACT (teacher adjusts instruction, students adjust learning)
    -> 5. Return to Step 2 (continuous cycle)
```

Must be rapid. Best formative assessment happens within a lesson.

## Common Techniques Quick Reference

| Technique | Time | Best For |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Exit ticket | 2-3 min | End-of-lesson check |
| Hinge question | 1-2 min | Mid-lesson decision point |
| Mini whiteboard | 1 min | Quick whole-class check |
| Think-pair-share | 3-5 min | Activating all learners |
| Traffic lights | 30 sec | Quick confidence gauge |
| Random calling | Ongoing | Universal participation |
| One-minute paper | 1-2 min | "What's muddiest?" |
