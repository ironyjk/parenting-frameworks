# Formative Assessment Skill

> "When the cook tastes the soup, that's formative assessment. When the customer tastes the soup, that's summative assessment." — Paul Black

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## Execution Protocol

### 1. Clarify the Target
State learning intention (what will you LEARN, not what will you DO) and success criteria (how will you know). Share BEFORE the lesson.

### 2. Elicit Evidence
Choose technique(s) based on context:

| Need | Technique | Time |
|------|-----------|------|
| End-of-lesson check | **Exit ticket** | 2-3 min |
| Mid-lesson decision | **Hinge question** (MC, each wrong answer = specific misconception) | 1-2 min |
| Quick whole-class | **Mini whiteboards** (all show simultaneously) | 1 min |
| Activate all learners | **Think-pair-share** (enforce the silence in "think") | 3-5 min |
| Confidence gauge | **Traffic lights** (green/yellow/red) | 30 sec |
| Universal participation | **Random calling** (if "I don't know," scaffold + return) | ongoing |

### 3. Interpret the Evidence
Sort responses: got it / partially / not yet. Identify specific misconceptions.

### 4. Act on the Evidence
- Teacher: re-teach, extend, differentiate, change approach
- Students: seek help, practice more, try new strategy
- **Act quickly** — if you collect Monday and adjust Friday, four days lost

### 5. Provide Feedback That Moves Forward
- **Specific:** "Your thesis is clear but paragraph 2 lacks evidence"
- **Actionable:** Tell them what to DO next
- **Timely:** Minutes to days, not weeks
- **Task-focused:** "This paragraph needs..." not "You're a weak writer"
- **Limit to 1-3 points** — more overwhelms

**Avoid:** Grades alone, grades + comments (students ignore comments), vague praise.

### 6. Activate Peer and Self-Assessment
- **Peer:** Two Stars and a Wish, Gallery Walk, C3B4ME
- **Self:** Assess against same rubric teacher uses, learning journals, goal tracking

---

## Anti-patterns

1. **Graded quizzes called "formative"** — if it counts toward the grade, it's summative
2. **Collecting but not acting** — exit tickets filed without informing next lesson
3. **Only checking hand-raisers** — 3 students answer; teacher assumes class gets it
4. **Feedback as grades** — numbers without guidance
5. **Delayed feedback** — 3 weeks later, learning moment passed
6. **Feedback overload** — 15 comments no one reads
7. **Self-assessment without criteria** — "How do you think you did?" with nothing to evaluate against
8. **Punishing wrong answers** — students hide misunderstandings
9. **One technique for everything** — vary the tools
10. **Skipping Strategy 1** — without learning intentions, nothing to assess against
