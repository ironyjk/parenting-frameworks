# Understanding by Design (UbD) — Theoretical Foundation

## Overview

Understanding by Design (UbD) is a curriculum planning framework by Grant Wiggins and Jay McTighe that reverses the traditional approach. Instead of starting with activities, it begins with the end: What should students understand? How will we know they understand? Only then plan activities. This "backward design" ensures alignment between goals, assessments, and instruction.

---

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: Identify Desired Results

Four levels of priority:
- **Transfer Goals** — long-term, independent use of learning
- **Enduring Understanding (Big Ideas)** — core, transferable concepts
- **Important to know and do** — key content and skills
- **Worth being familiar with** — context and details

**Big Ideas** have enduring value, reside at the heart of the discipline, require "uncoverage," and engage students.

**Essential Questions** are open-ended, have no single answer, raise further questions, and are worth revisiting.

| Topical (closed) | Essential (open) |
|---|---|
| "What caused WWI?" | "Why do nations go to war?" |
| "What is photosynthesis?" | "How do organisms get energy to survive?" |

### Stage 2: Determine Acceptable Evidence

Design assessments BEFORE lessons.

**Performance Tasks (GRASPS):**
- **G**oal — real-world problem
- **R**ole — student's role in scenario
- **A**udience — beyond the teacher
- **S**ituation — realistic context with constraints
- **P**roduct — tangible deliverable
- **S**tandards — rubric criteria

### Stage 3: Plan Learning Experiences

Only NOW plan activities, directly serving Stages 1 and 2.

## Six Facets of Understanding

| Facet | Prompt |
|-------|--------|
| **Explain** | "Why/how does this work?" |
| **Interpret** | "What does this mean?" |
| **Apply** | "How/when can I use this?" |
| **Perspective** | "From whose viewpoint? What alternatives?" |
| **Empathize** | "What would it feel like?" |
| **Self-Knowledge** | "What are my blind spots?" |

Robust assessment targets multiple facets.

## WHERETO: Learning Plan Design

| Element | Question |
|---------|----------|
| **W** | Where is the unit going? Why? |
| **H** | How will you hook and sustain interest? |
| **E** | What experiences will equip students? |
| **R** | How will students reflect and revise? |
| **E** | How will they self-evaluate? |
| **T** | How will you tailor to individuals? |
| **O** | How will it be sequenced? |

---

## Common Misunderstandings

| Misunderstanding | Clarification |
|------------------|---------------|
| "Teaching to the test" | UbD designs authentic tasks, not test prep |
| "Start with standards" | Standards inform Stage 1 but aren't Big Ideas |
| "Understanding = knowing" | Knowing is necessary but insufficient; understanding requires transfer |
| "Activities come first" | Most common error; activities must serve Stages 1 and 2 |

---

## Sample Unit Sketch: Nutrition (Grade 6)

**Stage 1:** Big Idea: food choices affect body systems. EQ: "How does what I eat affect how I feel?"
**Stage 2 (GRASPS):** You are a nutritionist redesigning the school cafeteria menu — healthier, within budget, appealing to students.
**Stage 3:** Personal food diary analysis -> nutrient lessons -> peer review of drafts -> final presentation.
