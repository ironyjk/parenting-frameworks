# Understanding by Design (UbD) Skill

> "The best designs derive backward from the learnings sought." — Grant Wiggins & Jay McTighe

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## Execution Protocol: Three-Stage Backward Design

### Stage 1: Desired Results

```
Transfer Goals:
  Students will independently use their learning to...

Meaning:
  Understandings:                    Essential Questions:
  Students will understand that...   [Open-ended, no single answer,
  [Big Idea as full sentence]        recur across contexts]

Acquisition:
  Knowledge:                         Skills:
  Students will know...              Students will be able to...
```

### Stage 2: Evidence (design BEFORE activities)

```
Performance Task (GRASPS):
  Goal:       [What must be accomplished]
  Role:       [Student's role]
  Audience:   [Who receives the product]
  Situation:  [Context and constraints]
  Product:    [What is created]
  Standards:  [Evaluation criteria]

Other Evidence:
  [Quiz, test, prompt, observation, self-assessment]
```

### Stage 3: Learning Plan (WHERETO)

```
  W: [How goals are communicated]
  H: [Hook — entry event or provocative question]
  E: [Key learning activities]
  R: [Reflection, revision, rethinking]
  E: [Formative assessment checkpoints]
  T: [Differentiation strategies]
  O: [Sequence and organization]
```

---

## Anti-patterns

1. **Activity-first design** — planning fun activities then retrofitting goals
2. **Coverage mentality** — "covering" everything instead of "uncovering" Big Ideas
3. **Assessment as afterthought** — designing the test last
4. **Topical questions only** — factual questions but not Essential Questions
5. **Single-facet assessment** — testing only recall without transfer or perspective
6. **Ignoring Stage 2** — jumping from goals directly to activities
7. **Confusing activity with learning** — "Students will make a poster" is not a learning goal
8. **Essential Questions that aren't** — closed, factual, too narrow
9. **One-shot design** — not iterating after teaching
10. **Standards as understanding** — compliance is not understanding

## Output Format

```
## UbD Unit Design

**Topic:** [subject and grade]

### Stage 1 — Desired Results
**Transfer Goal:** [long-term independent use]
**Big Idea(s):** [enduring understanding as full sentence]
**Essential Question(s):** [open-ended, inquiry-driving]
**Knowledge:** [key facts/terms]
**Skills:** [assessable abilities]

### Stage 2 — Evidence
**Performance Task (GRASPS):**
  G: [goal] | R: [role] | A: [audience]
  S: [situation] | P: [product] | S: [standards]
**Other Evidence:** [quizzes, observations, reflections]

### Stage 3 — Learning Plan (WHERETO)
[Sequenced activities serving Stages 1 and 2]
```
