# Attachment-Based Parenting Skill

> "What is most important is not what happens to us, but how we make sense of what happens to us." — Daniel Siegel

## The 4 S's in Action

```
Child:  [age 6, wakes up from nightmare]
Parent: [opens arms] "Hey, come here. Bad dream?" [SAFE]
Child:  [nods, climbs into lap, shaking]
Parent: "That must have been really scary." [SEEN]
         [holds child, speaks softly]
         "I've got you. You're safe. Just breathe with me." [SOOTHED]
Child:  [gradually stops shaking]
Parent: "Want to tell me about it?"
Child:  "A monster was chasing me and I couldn't run."
Parent: "That sounds terrifying. Dreams like that feel SO real." [SEEN]
         "I'm right here — always. Want me to sit with you 
         until you fall asleep?" [SECURE]
```

## Repair: The Most Important Attachment Skill

**Ruptures are inevitable.** Securely attached pairs experience just as many ruptures as insecure pairs. The difference is that secure pairs REPAIR.

### Repair Conversation Protocol

**When:** After you've calmed down. Same day when possible.

```
Parent: "Hey, can we talk about what happened earlier?"
Parent: "I yelled at you about the spilled milk, and that wasn't okay. 
         You made a mistake, and I overreacted."
         [OWN your behavior — no "but you should have..."]
Child:  "It scared me."
Parent: "I'm sorry I scared you. You don't deserve to be yelled at 
         for an accident." [VALIDATE their experience]
Child:  "I thought you were really mad at me."
Parent: "I was frustrated — but not at you. I was tired and I took it 
         out on you. That's MY mistake."
         [TAKE responsibility — differentiate trigger from child]
Parent: "Next time I'm that frustrated, I'm going to take a breath 
         before I say anything. Can you help remind me? You can say 
         'Dad, breathe.'" [PLAN for next time — collaborative]
```

### What Repair Teaches
- Relationships can survive conflict
- It's safe to express hurt
- Adults make mistakes and take responsibility
- The child is more important than being "right"

## Anti-patterns

1. **Confusing attachment parenting with permissive parenting** — secure attachment includes boundaries ("bigger, stronger, wiser, AND kind")
2. **Believing attachment is set in stone** — attachment can be earned at any age
3. **Pursuing perfection** — 30-50% attunement with consistent repair is sufficient
4. **Using attachment to guilt-trip** — "You'll damage attachment if you go to work" is a misuse
5. **Ignoring your own attachment history** — unresolved patterns replicate unless made conscious
6. **Repair without change** — "I'm sorry" without behavioral change is not repair
7. **Always safe haven, never secure base** — overprotection inhibits exploration
8. **Treating avoidant children as "easy"** — they're not fine; they've learned to suppress
