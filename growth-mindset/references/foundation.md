# Growth Mindset — Theoretical Foundation

## Overview

Growth mindset is the belief that intelligence, talent, and abilities are not fixed traits but can be developed through effort, effective strategies, and input from others. Developed by Stanford psychologist Carol Dweck through decades of research.

---

## Fixed vs. Growth Mindset

| Dimension | Fixed | Growth |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Core belief** | Intelligence is static | Intelligence can be developed |
| **Goal** | Look smart at all costs | Learn and grow at all costs |
| **Challenges** | Avoid (might reveal inadequacy) | Embrace (opportunities to grow) |
| **Effort** | Pointless if you're smart | Essential path to mastery |
| **Obstacles** | Give up easily | Persist through setbacks |
| **Criticism** | Ignore useful feedback | Learn from criticism |
| **Others' success** | Feel threatened | Find inspiration |
| **Failure** | "I AM a failure" (identity) | "I FAILED at this" (event) |

### The Danger
Fixed mindset works until it doesn't — when a naturally talented person hits their first real challenge and collapses.

---

## The Power of "Yet"

| Fixed | Growth |
|-------|--------|
| "I can't do math" | "I can't do math **yet**" |
| "I don't understand" | "I don't understand **yet**" |

"Yet" acknowledges reality (honest), implies trajectory (hopeful), and normalizes process (patient).

---

## Praise: The Most Powerful Lever

**Person Praise (AVOID):** "You're so smart!" "You're a natural!" "You're the best!"
**Process Praise (USE):** "You worked hard on that." "You tried a new technique." "You didn't give up."

### Process Praise = Effort + Strategy + Progress

1. **Effort:** "You put a lot of thought into this"
2. **Strategy:** "Breaking the problem into parts was smart"
3. **Progress:** "Compare this to September — look how much you've grown"

**Critical nuance:** Effort ALONE is insufficient. "Great effort!" to a student using an ineffective strategy teaches that trying is enough. It's not. Pair with strategy guidance.

## Brain Neuroplasticity (for teaching children)

- "Every time you learn something new, your brain makes new connections"
- "When you practice something hard, your brain grows — just like muscles"
- "Mistakes are when your brain is growing the most"
- Neurons that fire together wire together
- Myelin wraps around frequently used pathways (dirt road -> highway)
- Struggle activates deeper processing; easy tasks don't build connections

## False Growth Mindset (Common Traps)

### Trap 1: "Just Try Harder"
Growth mindset = effort + effective strategies + seeking help. Not effort alone.

### Trap 2: Praising Everything
Poor work doesn't deserve praise. "I can see you worked hard. The result isn't there yet. Let's find a different approach."

### Trap 3: Banning Fixed Mindset
Everyone has triggers. The goal is to recognize and shift, not pretend.

### Trap 4: Dismissing Systemic Barriers
"Just have a growth mindset" doesn't solve poverty or discrimination. Provide equitable opportunities AND foster growth mindset.

### Trap 5: Fixed Mindset About Mindset
"I'm a fixed-mindset person" is itself a fixed-mindset statement.

---

## The Growth Mindset Feedback Loop

```
Challenge encountered
  -> Fixed trigger: "I can't"
  -> RECOGNITION: "I notice I'm in fixed mindset"
  -> REFRAME: "Hard means growth. What strategy can I try?"
  -> EFFORT + STRATEGY: Try specific approach
  -> REFLECT: "What worked? What didn't?"
  -> GROWTH: Skill improves
  -> Next challenge with more resilience
```
