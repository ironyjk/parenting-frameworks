# Growth Mindset Skill

> "Becoming is better than being." — Carol Dweck

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## Execution Protocol

### 1. Identify the Mindset Trigger
What is the child/person saying or doing that signals fixed mindset?
- "I can't do this" / "This is too hard" / "I'm stupid"
- Giving up, avoiding challenge, hiding mistakes

### 2. Respond with Growth Reframe

| Child says... | Fixed response (avoid) | Growth response (use) |
|-------|------------------------|----------------------|
| "I can't do this" | "Yes you can, you're smart" | "What part is tricky? Let's break it down" |
| "This is too hard" | "It's not that hard" | "Hard means your brain is growing. What have you tried?" |
| "I made a mistake" | "That's okay, no big deal" | "Mistakes are information. What did this teach you?" |
| "She's better than me" | "No she's not" | "What does she do that you'd like to learn?" |
| "I got an A!" | "You're so smart!" | "You earned that! What study strategy worked best?" |
| "I failed the test" | "Maybe this isn't your subject" | "What will you do differently? Let's make a plan" |

### 3. Use Process Praise (Effort + Strategy + Progress)

| Person Praise (AVOID) | Process Praise (USE) |
|---|---|
| "You're so smart!" | "You worked really hard on that!" |
| "You're a natural!" | "I can see you practiced — your improvement shows" |
| "You're the best reader" | "You figured out that word by breaking it into parts — great strategy!" |

### 4. Teach About the Brain
- "Your brain makes new connections when you learn"
- "Practice makes brain pathways stronger — like upgrading a road"
- "Struggle is when your brain grows the most"

### 5. For Teachers
- Normalize struggle: "This is supposed to be hard"
- Post "Favorite Mistake of the Day"
- Share your own growth stories
- Create a "Yet" wall: things we can't do YET -> Now I Can

## Growth Mindset Self-Assessment

1. You receive critical feedback -> **Fixed:** defend, dismiss | **Growth:** curious, "what can I learn?"
2. Colleague solves what you couldn't -> **Fixed:** threatened | **Growth:** "how did they approach it?"
3. Asked to do something new -> **Fixed:** "what if I fail?" | **Growth:** "this will stretch me"
4. Child says "I'm stupid" -> **Fixed:** "No you're not!" | **Growth:** "You're struggling with this particular thing. That's different."

---

## Anti-patterns

1. **Person praise** — "You're so smart" creates fragile motivation
2. **Effort-only praise** — without strategy guidance
3. **Dismissing struggle** — "It's easy, just try"
4. **Labeling learners** — "gifted student" / "slow learner"
5. **Punishing mistakes** — creates fear of failure
6. **Comparing to others** — "Why can't you be like your sister?"
7. **Protecting from challenge** — easy work builds fragility, not confidence
8. **Growth mindset as toxic positivity** — forcing positivity without acknowledging difficulty
9. **Ignoring systemic factors** — mindset is not a substitute for equitable resources
10. **One-time intervention** — requires sustained practice, not a single lecture
