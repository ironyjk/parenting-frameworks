# Developmentally Appropriate Practice (DAP) Skill

> "Each child is a unique person with an individual pattern and timing of growth." — NAEYC

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## DAP Decision-Making Framework

When making any educational decision, ask:

1. **Is this appropriate for children this age?** (Age-appropriateness)
2. **Is this appropriate for THIS child?** (Individual-appropriateness)
3. **Is this appropriate in this child's cultural context?** (Cultural-appropriateness)
4. **Does it challenge AND support?** (Vygotsky's ZPD: what the child can do with support today, they do independently tomorrow)
5. **Does the evidence support it?** (Research-based, not tradition or convenience)

---

## Quick Reference: Age-Appropriate Expectations

### 0-3 Years
- Responsive caregiving is the PRIMARY need
- Rich language (narrate, sing, read)
- Safe spaces for movement exploration
- Sensory-rich materials, predictable routines

### 3-5 Years
- Play IS the primary learning vehicle (min 60 min free play daily)
- Dramatic play, emergent literacy, math through routines
- Social problem-solving coaching
- DO NOT push formal academics (worksheets, drills)

### 5-8 Years
- Active, hands-on learning (not just seat work)
- Collaborative projects and group work
- Recess is not optional
- Choice and voice, even within structured curriculum
- Assessment through observation, not just tests

---

## Anti-patterns

1. **Pushing academics too early** — worksheets for preschoolers (counterproductive)
2. **Eliminating play/recess** — removing the primary learning mechanism
3. **Same expectations for all** — ignoring developmental variation
4. **Cultural blindness** — "We treat all children the same" ignores context
5. **Deficit thinking about families** — focus on what families contribute
6. **Assessment through testing only** — young children show knowledge through action
7. **Rigid scheduling** — no room for child-initiated exploration
8. **Ignoring the body** — expecting young children to sit still for hours
9. **One-size-fits-all transitions** — every child must nap/sit in circle/participate simultaneously
10. **Prioritizing compliance over engagement** — quiet is not necessarily learning
