# Emotion Coaching — Theoretical Foundation

## Overview

Emotion Coaching is a parenting approach developed by Dr. John Gottman based on decades of research at the Gottman Institute. Through observing parent-child interactions and tracking long-term outcomes, Gottman found that children whose parents practiced Emotion Coaching had better physical health, higher academic achievement, stronger friendships, fewer behavioral problems, and greater emotional resilience.

The core insight: **emotions are not the problem; behavior might be.** All feelings are acceptable. Not all behaviors are. The parent's job is to be a guide through the child's emotional landscape.

---

## Four Parenting Meta-Emotion Styles

### 1. Dismissing Parent
**Belief:** Negative emotions are harmful or unimportant.
**Behaviors:** Ignores, trivializes. "It's not a big deal" / "You're fine."
**Impact:** Child learns emotions are wrong; difficulty regulating.

### 2. Disapproving Parent
**Belief:** Negative emotions are a sign of weakness.
**Behaviors:** Criticizes, punishes emotional expression. "Stop crying or I'll give you something to cry about."
**Impact:** Shame about emotions; may become aggressive or shut down.

### 3. Laissez-Faire Parent
**Belief:** All emotions and behaviors should be freely expressed.
**Behaviors:** Accepts emotions (good!) but fails to set limits or guide problem-solving.
**Impact:** Difficulty self-regulating; no guidance on what to DO with feelings.

### 4. Emotion Coaching Parent
**Belief:** Negative emotions are an opportunity for intimacy and teaching.
**Behaviors:** Aware of emotions, validates, labels, THEN sets limits and helps problem-solve.
**Impact:** Trust in feelings, better self-soothing, stronger relationships, higher achievement.

## Emotion Vocabulary by Age

| Age Range | Vocabulary Level | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 2-3 years | Basic 4: happy, sad, mad, scared | "You look mad. You wanted that toy." |
| 4-5 years | +frustrated, worried, excited, lonely, jealous, embarrassed | "It sounds like you're frustrated because you can't make it work." |
| 6-8 years | +disappointed, anxious, overwhelmed, left out, proud, confused, guilty | "Are you feeling disappointed because you expected to win?" |
| 9-12 years | +humiliated, betrayed, insecure, conflicted, resentful | "It sounds like you feel betrayed — like a trust was broken." |
| 13+ years | Full adult vocabulary + nuance, ambivalence, mixed emotions | "You seem both relieved and sad — two feelings at once." |

## When NOT to Emotion Coach

1. **Immediate safety threat** — "STOP!" not "I can see you're excited"
2. **You are flooded** — Stabilize yourself first
3. **Time pressure / wrong setting** — Acknowledge briefly, schedule talk
4. **The child needs space** — "I'm here when you're ready to talk"
5. **Repeated low-stakes situations** — Simple acknowledgment suffices

## Building Your Own Emotional Awareness

**Daily practice:**
1. Three times a day, pause: "What am I feeling right now?"
2. Name it specifically (not "fine" or "stressed")
3. Notice where you feel it in your body
4. Notice what triggered it

**Your own emotional history matters.** If your parents were Dismissing or Disapproving, you may instinctively replicate that.
