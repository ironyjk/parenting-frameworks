# Montessori Method Skill

> "The child is both a hope and a promise for mankind." — Maria Montessori

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## Execution Protocol

### 1. Identify the Sensitive Period
Is the child showing intense, repeated interest in something? (Order, Language, Movement, Sensory, Small objects, Social behavior)

### 2. Assess the Environment
- Is it child-sized and accessible?
- Orderly, beautiful, uncluttered?
- Real materials (not plastic/pretend)?
- Open-ended rather than single-use?

### 3. Observe Before Acting
Watch to understand the child's current stage, interests, and readiness. Do not interrupt concentration.

### 4. Connect Child to Environment
Introduce materials through the Three-Period Lesson:
1. **Name:** "This is ___."
2. **Recognize:** "Show me ___." (most time here)
3. **Recall:** "What is this?" (only when ready)

### 5. Step Back
The goal is for the child to not need you. Protect concentration. Resist the urge to praise, correct, or redirect a focused child.

## Quick Reference: Curriculum Areas and Key Materials

| Area | Purpose | Key Materials/Activities |
|------|---------|------------------------|
| **Practical Life** | Concentration, coordination, independence | Pouring, buttoning, food prep, sweeping, grace & courtesy |
| **Sensorial** | Refine senses, observe, compare, categorize | Pink Tower, Brown Stair, Color Tablets, Sound Cylinders |
| **Language** | Oral -> writing -> reading | Sandpaper letters, movable alphabet, I Spy |
| **Mathematics** | Concrete to abstract | Golden beads, number rods, stamp game, bead chains |
| **Cultural** | Interconnected human knowledge | Puzzle maps, botany, zoology, timelines, art, music |

## Anti-patterns

1. **Adult-directed activities** — telling children what to work on and when
2. **Interrupting concentration** — "What a beautiful tower!" breaks flow
3. **Rewards and punishments** — stickers/prizes undermine intrinsic motivation
4. **Teaching before readiness** — pushing materials before interest shows
5. **Plastic/pretend over real** — toy brooms instead of real brooms
6. **Cluttered environment** — too many materials, visual noise
7. **Same-age grouping** — loses mentoring and community benefits
8. **Short work periods** — 30-minute blocks prevent deep concentration
9. **Correcting errors directly** — let self-correcting materials teach
10. **Confusing with permissiveness** — freedom within limits, not without structure
