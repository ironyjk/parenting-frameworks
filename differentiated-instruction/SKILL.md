# Differentiated Instruction Skill

> "Fair isn't always equal, and equal isn't always fair." — Rick Wormeli

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## Execution Protocol

### 1. Clarify KUD (same for ALL students)
- **Know:** Facts, vocabulary, definitions
- **Understand:** Big Ideas, transferable truths
- **Do:** Observable, verifiable skills

### 2. Pre-Assess
Determine where each student is relative to the KUD.

### 3. Differentiate by Element + Characteristic

| | Readiness | Interest | Learning Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Content** | Varied reading levels | Choice of subtopics | Multiple media formats |
| **Process** | Tiered activities | Interest-based groups | Visual/verbal/kinesthetic options |
| **Product** | Complexity tiers | Choice of demonstration | Multiple formats allowed |
| **Environment** | Scaffolded zones | Interest corners | Quiet vs. collaborative spaces |

### 4. Apply Strategies

**Tiered Assignments:** Design on-grade version first, then scaffold down (approaching) and open up (advanced). Same KUD, different pathways.

**Flexible Grouping:** Change groups with the task and the data. Never permanent.

**Choice Menus:** Structured options all addressing the same KUD. Student picks the path.

**Compacting:** For students who already know it — pre-assess, document mastery, replace with enrichment (not more of the same).

**Anchor Activities:** Meaningful independent work for transitions and early finishers.

---

## Anti-patterns

1. **Teaching to the middle** — ignoring top and bottom
2. **Permanent groups** — labeling students all year
3. **More of the same** — 20 more problems instead of different/deeper work
4. **Differentiating everything** — be strategic to avoid burnout
5. **Lowering standards** — adjust the PATH, not the DESTINATION
6. **Differentiating without data** — guessing instead of pre-assessing
7. **Ignoring interests** — readiness-only differentiation misses motivation
8. **Making it visible** — if students can identify "the easy group," it's stigmatizing
9. **No anchor activities** — teacher can't work small groups without them
10. **Doing it alone** — sustainable when teachers collaborate
