# Collaborative & Proactive Solutions (CPS) — Theoretical Foundation

## Overview

Collaborative & Proactive Solutions (CPS), developed by Dr. Ross Greene, is a radically different approach to understanding and addressing challenging behavior in children. Its foundational philosophy replaces "kids do well if they want to" with **"kids do well if they can."**

Challenging behavior is not the result of poor motivation, bad parenting, or willful defiance. It is the result of **lagging skills** — the child lacks the cognitive skills (flexibility, frustration tolerance, problem-solving) to meet certain expectations. The solution is not to motivate harder (rewards/punishments) but to **build skills and solve problems collaboratively**.

---

## The Philosophy Shift

| Traditional Lens | CPS Lens |
|---|---|
| Kids do well if they **want** to | Kids do well if they **can** |
| Misbehavior = poor motivation | Challenging behavior = lagging skills + unsolved problems |
| Solution: motivate (reward/punish) | Solution: teach skills + solve problems collaboratively |
| The child is the problem | The mismatch between expectations and skills is the problem |
| "What consequence should I impose?" | "What skill is lacking? What problem needs solving?" |

---

## ALSUP: Assessment of Lagging Skills and Unsolved Problems

### Column 1: Lagging Skills

- **Flexibility / Adaptability** — difficulty with transitions, changes in plan
- **Frustration Tolerance** — low threshold, becomes overwhelmed easily
- **Problem-Solving** — difficulty thinking of multiple solutions
- **Emotional Regulation** — difficulty managing emotional response
- **Social Thinking** — difficulty understanding perspectives, reading cues
- **Language Processing** — difficulty expressing concerns in words
- **Cognitive Flexibility** — black-and-white thinking
- **Executive Functioning** — organization, planning, time management
- **Sensory/Emotional Sensitivity** — heightened sensitivity
- **Chronic Irritability** — persistent mood issues

### Column 2: Unsolved Problems

Must be **specific**, **observable**, and **free of adult theories**:

**Good:** "Difficulty getting off the tablet when screen time is over"
**Bad:** "Won't listen" (vague), "Is defiant" (label), "Refuses to cooperate" (judgment)

---

## The Three Plans

### Plan A: Impose Your Will
Adult imposes solution unilaterally. Increases challenging behavior. **Use only for genuine safety emergencies.**

### Plan B: Collaborate (THE CORE)
Solve the problem WITH the child. Three steps: Empathy -> Define -> Invite.

### Plan C: Drop It (For Now)
Intentionally set aside an expectation. NOT giving in — strategic prioritization.

**Choosing Plans:**
- List all unsolved problems
- Prioritize 2-3 for Plan B
- Place the rest in Plan C
- Plan A only for immediate safety

---

## CPS with Different Ages

**Ages 3-5:** Simpler language, offer choices, keep Plan B short.
**Ages 6-12:** Full Plan B. Children can articulate concerns with scaffolding.
**Ages 13-18:** Especially effective. Teens crave autonomy and respect.
