# Triple P Positive Parenting Program — Theoretical Foundation

## Overview

Triple P (Positive Parenting Program) was developed by Professor Matthew Sanders at the University of Queensland, Australia. It is one of the most extensively researched parenting programs in the world, with over 600 published studies across 30+ countries demonstrating its effectiveness.

Triple P is a **multi-level, public health approach** to parenting support. Rather than a single method, it provides a graduated system of interventions — from light-touch universal information to intensive family therapy — matched to the level of need.

**Note:** Triple P is a registered program name. This skill describes the evidence-based framework and principles for educational reference.

---

## The Five Levels

### Level 1: Universal (Information)
**Target:** All parents. **Format:** Media campaigns, tip sheets, brief information.

### Level 2: Selected (Brief Intervention)
**Target:** Parents with specific concerns. **Format:** 1-2 session seminars, brief consultations.

### Level 3: Primary Care (Narrow Focus)
**Target:** Specific behavioral concern requiring skills training. **Format:** 3-4 sessions focused on one or two behaviors.

### Level 4: Standard/Group (Broad Focus)
**Target:** Multiple behavioral concerns. **Format:** 8-10 sessions, comprehensive skills program.

### Level 5: Enhanced (Intensive Family Intervention)
**Target:** Severe issues PLUS family dysfunction. **Format:** Level 4 + partner support, mood management, stress coping.

---

## Five Core Positive Parenting Principles

### 1. Ensure a Safe and Engaging Environment
Childproof, age-appropriate, supervised without hovering, predictable routines.

### 2. Create a Positive Learning Environment
Incidental teaching, Ask-Say-Do, modeling behavior. Children learn best through positive engagement.

### 3. Use Assertive Discipline
Not aggressive, not passive. Clear, calm, consistent. Graduated response from descriptive praise to logical consequences.

### 4. Have Realistic Expectations
Understand developmental norms. Avoid perfectionism. Accept that ALL children misbehave.

### 5. Take Care of Yourself as a Parent
Identify stress triggers, develop coping strategies, address relationship issues, ask for help.

## The Self-Regulation Framework

Triple P's deepest layer: teaching parents to become their own behavior change agents.

### For Parents
1. **Self-sufficiency** — develop your own problem-solving skills
2. **Self-efficacy** — believe in your ability to manage challenges
3. **Self-management** — set personal goals, monitor your own behavior
4. **Personal agency** — recognize you can influence outcomes

### For Children
The ultimate goal: children who can regulate their own behavior, emotions, and attention without external control.

**Building self-regulation:**
- Give choices (autonomy builds self-regulation)
- Gradually reduce external scaffolding
- Teach self-monitoring: "How do you think that went?"
- Praise self-correction: "You started to grab it, then stopped and asked."
